Teaching

I teach the sociology of media and the digital at the School of Communication and the School of Journalism of Louvain (UCLouvain), from the bachelor's to the master's level. My courses start from concrete cases to question the role of information, the media and digital technologies in democratic life and public debate.

Ongoing courses

  • Contemporary Issues in Information and Communication

    LCOMU1124 · Bachelor's · Q1 · 4 credits · since 2019

    An introductory course in information and communication sciences. Through case studies, it analyses the digital transition of contemporary societies and its effects on democracy, the media industry and our practices of sociability.

  • Media Narratives and Journalism

    LCOMU1313 · Bachelor's · Q1 · 5 credits · since 2019

    A course in media narratology focusing on the role of media and journalists in the construction and framing of public problems.

  • Editorial Management of Diversity Issues

    Master's in journalism · since 2022

    Aimed at master's students in journalism, this course focuses on the issue of diversity in the media: diversity in the production of information as well as in the make-up of newsrooms. It is organised in three parts: gender, media and journalism; making intersectionality visible and mediated; and representing diversity — destigmatisation and objectivity.

  • Journalism and Environmental Issues

    Master's · since 2023

    A reflection on the journalistic coverage of ecological and climate crises, between the constraints of the profession, the formats of news, and responsibility in the face of transitions.

  • Digital Culture and Practices

    With Sandrine Roginsky · UCLouvain · since 2023

    A co-taught course on digital cultures and the everyday uses of technology, combining a sociological approach with the analysis of practices.

  • University Skills I

    LCOMU1105 · Bachelor's · Q1+Q2 · 6 credits · with Thibault Philippette

    An introduction to the methods and tools of university work in information and communication, from documentary research to the formalisation of scientific reasoning.

  • University Work Seminar in Information and Communication

    LCOMU1125 · Bachelor's · Q1 · 2 credits

    A seminar on the critical assessment of sources — written, oral, visual, numerical — and the construction of rigorous scientific reasoning.
